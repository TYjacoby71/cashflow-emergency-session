Introduction

How to Use This Playbook

This is not a strategy document. It's an execution toolkit. Every section is designed to be read once and acted on immediately.

This playbook works if you have at least one of these:

  • An existing offer (service, product, or expertise)
  • A list — email subscribers, past buyers, social followers, or a CRM
  • Partners, affiliates, or referral relationships (even dormant ones)
  • Inbound demand you haven't fully converted

This playbook will not help if:

  • You have no existing offer and no existing audience
  • You need a business model, not cashflow acceleration
  • You can't move fast — these plays require 2–4 hours of execution per day for 3 days
The three-step process:
Step 1 → Complete the Cash Play Selector (Chapter 1) to identify your best-fit play.
Step 2 → Read only that play's chapter (Chapters 2–6). Execute the launch checklist.
Step 3 → Run the 72-hour action map (Chapter 7) in parallel from Day 1.

A note on timing

The 72-hour window is real. Each play is designed to generate a response — a payment, a booked call, a confirmed order — within 72 hours of launch. If you're still in "research mode" at hour 24, you've misread the playbook. Launch imperfectly, iterate in real-time.

What each chapter contains

  • Chapter 1 — Cash Play Selector: A diagnostic matrix to pick your play based on your current assets
  • Chapters 2–6 — The 5 Plays: Full execution guide for each play, including scripts and checklists
  • Chapter 7 — 72-Hour Action Map: Day-by-day calendar with tasks, owners, and go/no-go checkpoints
  • Appendix A — AR & Expense Triage: Parallel cashflow levers to run alongside your play

Chapter 2 · Play 1

High-Ticket Partner Blitz

Play 1 of 5

High-Ticket Partner Blitz

Revive warm contacts, affiliates, and referral partners with a short, high-urgency outreach sequence. Target: $1,000–$10,000+ per deal.

Best for
  • Founders with an existing service business and a network of warm contacts
  • Anyone who has done a deal, referral, or collaboration in the past 18 months
  • People who have a high-value offer but haven't been actively selling
The mechanics

You're reactivating relationships, not cold-calling strangers. The play works because warm contacts already trust you — they just need a specific ask with a clear reason to act now.

Target list: 10–25 contacts. Prioritize: (1) Past clients who got results, (2) Referral partners who sent you business, (3) Warm prospects who went cold.

Launch checklist
  • Build your target list: 10–25 names, sorted by warmth (hottest first)
  • Write one sentence describing the specific value you're offering right now
  • Customize Message 1 for your top 5 contacts (send today)
  • Schedule follow-up Message 2 for non-responders (send Hour 30–36)
  • Send closing Message 3 to anyone who engaged but didn't book (Hour 60)
  • Have a booking link or phone number ready before you send Message 1
  • Track responses: Name / Sent / Response / Status

Scripts

Message 1 — Initial Outreach (send via SMS, DM, or personal email)
SCRIPT 1A · PAST CLIENTHey [Name] — quick one. I'm taking on 2 new [service type] clients this month with a specific focus on [specific outcome]. You came to mind immediately because of [specific thing you did together / result they got]. Are you in a position where this would be useful right now, or do you know someone who is? Either way — appreciate you. [Your name]
SCRIPT 1B · REFERRAL PARTNERHey [Name] — hope things are moving well on your end. I'm doing a targeted push this week and wanted to reach out personally. I'm looking for [specific type of client] who need [specific outcome] fast. 2–3 spots this month. You tend to know people in this situation. If anyone comes to mind, I'm happy to handle the conversation from there and make sure you're looked after. Worth a quick chat? [Your name]
SCRIPT 1C · WARM PROSPECT (WENT COLD)Hey [Name] — it's been a while. I'm not going to pretend otherwise. I've been thinking about the conversation we had about [topic]. I have something specific now that I think is a better fit for your situation — tighter scope, faster delivery, clearer outcome. Would it be worth 20 minutes this week to revisit? No pressure either way. [Your name]
Message 2 — Follow-Up (Hour 30–36 for non-responders)
SCRIPT 1D · FOLLOW-UPHey [Name] — just following up on my last message. I know things get busy. Still have 2 spots open. If the timing is off, totally understand. If there's something here — even a 10-minute call to see if it makes sense — I'm available [day] and [day] this week. [Your name]
Message 3 — Close or Release (Hour 60)
SCRIPT 1E · CLOSEHey [Name] — last message on this. I'm closing out these spots by end of week. If you want in, the next step is just [book a call / reply YES / click this link]. If not — no problem at all, I won't bring it up again. [Your name]
Urgency framing that works: "Closing spots by end of week" beats "limited availability" every time. Be specific. Have a real reason — even if that reason is "I want to start delivery this month."
Chapter 3 · Play 2

Automation Audit Upsell

Play 2 of 5

Automation Audit Upsell

Turn existing operator pain into an immediate fixed-fee service. Scoped deliverable, clear outcome, fast to sell. Target: $500–$3,000 per audit.

Best for
  • Agencies, consultants, or operators who work with business clients
  • Anyone with access to clients who run workflows (email, CRM, fulfillment, ops)
  • People who can identify process waste and propose a fix
The offer structure

The Automation Audit is a fixed-fee, scoped assessment. You're selling a specific deliverable: a documented map of where the client is leaking time or money, with prioritized recommendations.

  • Deliverable: Workflow audit report — 5–10 pages, prioritized findings
  • Timeline: 3–5 business days from kickoff
  • Price: $500–$3,000 depending on complexity and your positioning
  • Upsell path: "We can implement Fix #1 for [price] — want to start there?"
Launch checklist
  • Identify 3–5 current or past clients who could benefit (operators with messy workflows)
  • Set your audit price (start at $750 if unsure)
  • Write 1 sentence describing what pain the audit solves for them specifically
  • Send Message 2A to each client today
  • Prepare a simple intake form or questionnaire (5–8 questions) for kickoff
  • Draft a 1-page "Audit Overview" doc you can send when they say yes
  • Follow up with non-responders at Hour 36 using Message 2B

Scripts

Outreach to existing clients
SCRIPT 2A · CLIENT OUTREACHHey [Name] — quick question for you. We've been working together on [what you do], and I've noticed a pattern with operators at your stage: there are usually 2–3 workflow gaps that are costing time and revenue that nobody's looked at closely. I'm running a limited number of Automation Audits this month — a fixed-fee, 3–5 day assessment where I map your current workflow, flag the friction points, and give you a prioritized fix list. Price is $[X] flat. Deliverable is a clear action doc — not a proposal for more work. Would this be useful to you right now? Happy to tell you more if so. [Your name]
SCRIPT 2B · FOLLOW-UPHey [Name] — following up on my message about the Automation Audit. If timing is off, no problem. But if you've been meaning to clean up [specific workflow area], this is the fastest way to get a clear picture of where to start. I have [X] spots left this month. Reply with "interested" and I'll send the details. [Your name]
Use a real constraint: "I have 3 audit slots this month and they're filling" is true the moment you decide it is. Set a real capacity limit and honor it.
Audit report structure (what to deliver)
  1. Executive summary — 3 bullets: current state, main leak, top recommendation
  2. Workflow map — visual or written description of current process
  3. Findings — 5–8 friction points ranked by impact and ease of fix
  4. Priority fix list — top 3 actions with estimated time/cost to implement
  5. Next step offer — optional implementation engagement (your upsell)
Chapter 4 · Play 3

List + Offer Reactivation

Play 3 of 5

List + Offer Reactivation

Pull revenue from dormant leads, past buyers, or neglected communities with a sharper, time-limited ask. Target: $500–$5,000+ per campaign run.

Best for
  • Founders with an email list of 100+ subscribers (even cold)
  • Anyone with past buyers who haven't been re-engaged in 60+ days
  • Community owners, newsletter operators, or social creators with an audience
The mechanics

A dead list is not a dead asset. Lists go cold because the sender stopped showing up with value and clear offers. A 3-email reactivation sequence with a sharp offer can convert at 1–5% even on cold lists.

The math: 500 subscribers × 2% conversion × $97 offer = ~$970 in 72 hours. 1,000 subscribers × 2% × $297 offer = ~$5,940.

Launch checklist
  • Export and clean your list (remove hard bounces, duplicates)
  • Pick one offer — the simplest, most specific thing you can sell right now
  • Write the subject lines for all 3 emails before writing the body
  • Send Email 1 today — no preamble, no apologies
  • Send Email 2 at Hour 24 — a different angle, not a repeat
  • Send Email 3 at Hour 48 — hard close, segment after this
  • Have a payment link live before sending Email 1

Email Sequence Scripts

Email 1 — The Direct Offer (send today)
EMAIL 1 · SUBJECT: The fastest way to [outcome]Subject: The fastest way to [specific outcome] [First name] — I'll keep this short. I put together [offer name] for [specific type of person] who need [specific outcome] and want it working in [timeframe]. Here's what it includes: - [Specific benefit 1] - [Specific benefit 2] - [Specific benefit 3] Price: $[X] — access is instant. → [LINK] Get access now This is a short run. [Number] spots / closing [day]. — [Your name] P.S. If this isn't for you, feel free to ignore. If you know someone who needs [outcome], forward this.
Email 2 — Reframe (Hour 24)
EMAIL 2 · SUBJECT: Different angle on [topic]Subject: What most [audience type] get wrong about [topic] [First name] — Most [audience type] trying to [goal] focus on [wrong thing] and miss [right thing]. If you were on the fence after yesterday's email, here's the page with the full details: → [LINK] [X] spots remain. [Day] is the cutoff. — [Your name]
Email 3 — Hard Close (Hour 48)
EMAIL 3 · SUBJECT: Closing tonightSubject: Closing tonight — [offer name] [First name] — Last call. [Offer name] closes tonight at [time zone]. Subject line principles: Specificity beats cleverness. "The fastest way to recover $5k in AR" outperforms "A note from me." Test your subject lines by asking: "Would I open this if a stranger sent it?"
Chapter 5 · Play 4

Fast Upsell to Current Clients

Play 4 of 5

Fast Upsell to Current Clients

Sell something adjacent, faster, or deeper to people who already trust you. Target: $250–$5,000 per client.

Best for
  • Service providers with 2+ active clients
  • Founders with recent buyers (past 90 days)
  • Anyone who has an adjacent offer or can package one in 24 hours
The mechanics

Selling to existing clients costs zero in trust-building. The conversation starts at a completely different baseline. The key is having a specific, scoped offer — not a vague "I could also help with…"

Upsell offer types that close fast:

  • Acceleration add-on: "For $X, I'll do [faster/more] on what we're already doing"
  • Adjacent service: "I now offer [related service] — here's how it applies to you"
  • Done-for-you upgrade: "I can take this off your plate completely for $X/month"
  • Intensive session: "3-hour working session to solve [specific problem] — $X"
Launch checklist
  • List every current client and recent buyer (last 90 days)
  • For each, write 1 sentence: what are they struggling with right now?
  • Match a specific offer to that pain — don't pitch general capabilities
  • Send Message 4A by end of today — personal, not a broadcast
  • If they engage, send the specific offer in writing within 2 hours
  • Have a way to collect payment immediately (Stripe link, invoice)
  • Follow up once at Hour 36 if no response

Scripts

Initial upsell message
SCRIPT 4A · UPSELL PITCHHey [Name] — While we've been working on [current engagement], I noticed [specific observation about their situation — a gap, a risk, an opportunity]. I have something that addresses exactly that — [offer name in 5 words or less]. It's a [format: session / add-on / deliverable], scoped to [what it covers], priced at $[X]. Given where you are right now, I think it's worth it. Want me to send you the details? [Your name]
SCRIPT 4B · FOLLOW-UP (HOUR 36)Hey [Name] — following up on my message about [offer name]. I know you're busy. If the timing is off, let me know and I'll hold the spot until [date]. If you want to move forward, I can have a payment link to you in 10 minutes. [Your name]
When they say yes
  1. Send a Stripe payment link or invoice immediately — while they're warm
  2. Confirm the kickoff date in the same message
  3. Keep the confirmation email to 3–4 sentences maximum
The cardinal rule of upselling: Offer something specific, not something general. "I could help you with your email marketing" closes at 5%. "Here's a 3-email win-back sequence for your current drop-off problem, $400, done by Friday" closes at 40%+.
Chapter 6 · Play 5

Inbound Conversion Sprint

Play 5 of 5

Inbound Conversion Sprint

Work your unconverted inbound leads systematically — DMs, form fills, calls that went nowhere, and content engagement. Target: 1–3 deals from leads already in your pipeline.

Best for
  • Founders who generate inbound but let leads go cold
  • Anyone with 3+ unconverted leads in the last 30–60 days
  • People active on social or content who get DMs but don't follow up
The mechanics

Inbound leads are the warmest possible prospects — they came to you. Most founders let them go cold because there was no structured follow-through. This play fixes that with a 72-hour sprint through your existing pipeline.

Sources to mine:

  • Unanswered DMs or comments on social posts from last 30 days
  • Contact form submissions you haven't fully followed up on
  • Discovery calls that "went to follow up" and never closed
  • Email thread leads who went quiet after initial exchange
Launch checklist
  • List every unconverted inbound from the last 30–60 days
  • Score each: Hot (engaged, had a call), Warm (responded once), Cold (never responded)
  • Start with Hot leads — send Message 5A today
  • Send Warm leads Message 5B at Hour 12
  • Send Cold leads Message 5C at Hour 24
  • For anyone who engages: book a call or send a specific offer within 2 hours
  • Track every outreach: Name / Source / Status / Last Contact / Next Step

Scripts

Hot leads — had a call, went quiet
SCRIPT 5A · HOT LEAD FOLLOW-UPHey [Name] — I know it's been [timeframe] since we talked about [topic/their problem]. I'm not sure what happened on your end, but I wanted to reach back out because I think [specific reason: the timing might be better / I have something more targeted / this is about to close]. If you're still open to it, I'd love to pick up where we left off. Even just a quick 15-minute check-in. Here's my calendar: [link] [Your name]
Warm leads — responded but never booked
SCRIPT 5B · WARM LEAD REACTIVATIONHey [Name] — you reached out about [topic] a while back. Not sure if your situation has changed, but I'm in a position to help right now with [specific offer/outcome]. I have [X] spots available this month. If the timing is finally right, I'd love to talk. Happy to do a quick 20-minute call this week — here's a link: [link] If the timing still isn't right, no worries at all. Just wanted to follow through. [Your name]
Cold leads — contacted you but never engaged
SCRIPT 5C · COLD LEAD REACTIVATIONHey [Name] — you reached out about [topic / via your form] a while ago. I'm following up to see if you're still looking for help with [their stated problem or context clue]. If yes, I can make myself available this week. If the situation has changed, just let me know and I'll close this out. [Your name]
Note on cold leads: Script 5C is intentionally short. Long messages signal desperation. A cold lead who sees a short, confident message is more likely to re-engage than one who gets a three-paragraph sales pitch.
Chapter 7

72-Hour Action Map

This is your execution calendar. Run it in parallel with whichever play you selected. The map assumes you started at 8am on Day 1. Adjust times to your schedule but keep the sequence exactly as shown.

Go / No-Go rule: At the end of each day, run the check: "Did I send the outreach I planned? Did I respond to every lead within 2 hours?" If No on either — wake up earlier tomorrow. The play only works if you're faster than your leads' attention span.
Day 1 Launch & Seed
  • 8:00–9:00 YOU Build your target list (10–25 names for Plays 1–4; pipeline audit for Play 5). Quality list = quality results. Do not skip this step.
  • 9:00–10:00 YOU Customize Message 1 for your top 5 highest-priority contacts. Personalization takes 3 minutes per message — do not batch-send generic versions.
  • 10:00–11:00 YOU Send Message 1 to your full list. SMS/DM for warm contacts; email for list plays. Send individually, not BCC.
  • 11:00–12:00 YOU Confirm your payment infrastructure is live: Stripe link, invoice template, booking link. Test it. Don't let a broken checkout kill a sale.
  • 12:00–17:00 YOU Respond to every reply within 2 hours. Set a phone alarm. For any "interested" reply, send a specific offer or book a call within the same hour.
  • 17:00–18:00 YOU Day 1 debrief: log responses in your tracker. Count: Sent / Replied / Interested / Booked. Set up Day 2 follow-up list.
Day 2 Follow-Up & Convert
  • 8:00–9:00 YOU Review Day 1 tracker. Prioritize: (1) People who said "interested" but haven't paid, (2) People who engaged but didn't book, (3) Everyone who didn't reply.
  • 9:00–10:30 YOU Send Message 2 to all non-responders from Day 1. Use the follow-up scripts from your play chapter. 3–4 sentences max.
  • 10:30–12:00 YOU Conduct any calls booked from Day 1. Have a 1-page offer summary open during calls. Close on the call — do not say "I'll send you a proposal."
  • 12:00–13:00 YOU ⚑ GO / NO-GO CHECK: Have you sent at least 10 outreach messages? Have you received at least 2 replies? If not — diagnose now. Wrong list? Wrong message? Wrong timing?
  • 13:00–17:00 YOU Respond to all replies within 2 hours. For "yes" responses: collect payment immediately. For "maybe" responses: give them a deadline (tomorrow at 5pm).
  • 17:00–18:00 YOU Day 2 debrief: Update tracker. Log revenue collected. Set final follow-up list for Day 3.
Day 3 Close & Capture
  • 8:00–9:00 YOU Review Day 2 tracker. Build your Day 3 close list: everyone who engaged but hasn't paid. These are your highest-leverage conversations today.
  • 9:00–10:30 YOU Send Message 3 (hard close) to all "maybe" or unresponsive contacts. Use the close scripts from your play chapter. Be direct — this is the last message.
  • 10:30–14:00 YOU Conduct any remaining calls. Close hard. If someone wants to "think about it" — give them a 2-hour window before the offer closes. That's respect for both your time.
  • 14:00–16:00 YOU Final payment collection push. Follow up on any outstanding payment links. Send a "link expires at 5pm" message to everyone who clicked but didn't pay.
  • 16:00–17:00 YOU Close the campaign. Stop outreach. Log final numbers: Total contacted / Replied / Calls held / Deals closed / Revenue collected.
  • 17:00–18:00 YOU Post-mortem: Which play worked? What would you do differently? What's the next play you'll run? Document while it's fresh.
After 72 hours: Move anyone who didn't convert to a long-nurture list. Do not keep following up past Day 3. Your next campaign with them starts fresh, not as a continuation of this one.
Appendix A

Immediate Cashflow Levers: AR & Expense Triage

While you run your chosen play, work these two levers in parallel. They don't require outreach — just action on things that are already yours.

Accounts Receivable Acceleration

If you have outstanding invoices, this is the fastest cash available to you — faster than any new sale.

AR triage checklist
  • Pull a full AR report right now: who owes you, how much, and how overdue
  • Identify any invoice 14+ days overdue — contact those clients today, not tomorrow
  • Offer a 2% early-pay discount to clients 30–60 days out if you need cash now
  • For clients 60+ days overdue: call first, then email. A 2-minute call resolves more than 3 emails
  • Switch all future invoices to Net 7 or Net 14 (not Net 30+)
  • Add a late payment clause to contracts going forward (1.5%/month is standard)
  • Use Stripe or similar to accept card — don't gate payment on bank transfers alone
AR RECOVERY CALL SCRIPTHey [Name] — quick call about invoice #[X] for $[amount], which shows outstanding as of [date]. I just want to make sure everything is OK on your end and nothing got lost in transit. Can we sort out payment today? I can resend the invoice, set up a payment plan, or take a card over the phone — whatever is easiest for you. [Listen. Agree on a specific date and method. Confirm in writing same day.]

Expense Triage Framework

Every dollar you don't spend is a dollar you don't have to earn. Run this triage in parallel with your play — it takes 2 hours and extends your runway immediately.

The 3-bucket sort

Pull every recurring expense from your bank or accounting tool. Sort into three buckets:

  • Bucket 1 — Revenue-Generating: Tools or spend directly tied to making sales (CRM, ad spend, core SaaS). Keep.
  • Bucket 2 — Operational (essential): Payroll, hosting, core infrastructure. Keep, but look for cheaper alternatives at renewal.
  • Bucket 3 — Nice-to-Have: Everything else. Cancel or pause today.
Typical Bucket 3 finds: Unused SaaS subscriptions, duplicate tools (2 project managers, 2 analytics platforms), premium plans where free tiers work fine, conference/event commitments that can be deferred, agency retainers on pause.
Expense triage checklist
  • Export every recurring charge from bank statements (last 30 days)
  • Tag each as Bucket 1, 2, or 3
  • Cancel all Bucket 3 items today — do not wait for the billing cycle
  • Negotiate or downgrade all Bucket 2 items at next renewal
  • Calculate your new monthly burn — update your runway estimate
  • Set a calendar reminder to repeat this triage in 30 days
The 30-day rule: If you haven't actively used a tool in 30 days, cancel it. You can always re-subscribe. The cost of missing it for one month is almost always lower than the cost of the subscription itself.
